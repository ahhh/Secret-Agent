# SECRET AGENT: FIELD OPERATIONS

*A covert social mission game for conventions, festivals, campuses, and public events*

---

# Game Premise

Players become undercover field agents operating in plain sight. Each agent receives a secret mission that must be completed without exposing the mission, the game, or their identity as an active agent. Agents gather evidence, avoid detection, and attempt to complete objectives while surrounded by civilians and rival agents.

---

# Core Design Goals

- Encourage social interaction and improvisation
- Create paranoia and stealth without requiring props or technology
- Reward subtlety over speed
- Support emergent storytelling and bluffing
- Allow spectators to unknowingly become part of the game environment
- Prevent mission leakage and metagaming

---

# Core Concepts

**Agent:** A player currently participating.

**Handler:** The organizer/referee who distributes missions and resolves disputes.

**Handle:** The chosen alias an agent registers when accepting a mission. Used for scoring, leaderboards, and the Hall of Fame. Handles must be unique per game instance.

**Mission Card:** A hidden objective assigned to an agent.

**Cover:** The believable social persona an agent maintains while completing missions.

**Exposure:** When another player successfully identifies or proves that an agent is playing Secret Agent.

**Evidence:** A photo, witness confirmation, or physical proof of mission completion.

**Dead Drop:** A predetermined method or location for turning in evidence anonymously.

**Mission Intake:** The process by which an agent receives a mission and registers their handle, typically via QR code.

---

# Starting the Game

## Mission Intake via QR Code (Standard Method)

1. The Handler places or displays one or more Mission Intake QR codes at the event.
2. A player scans the QR code on their personal device.
3. The intake page generates a random mission from the active mission pool.
4. The player enters their desired **handle** (a unique alias for the game).
5. The player accepts the mission. This registers their handle and locks in their assigned mission.
6. The player receives a mission briefing on-screen which they should read and then dismiss — the mission should not be saved, screenshot, or shown to others.
7. The Handler receives a notification or log entry confirming the agent has been registered.

**Important:** Scanning the QR code a second time after accepting a mission does not grant a new mission. Each registered handle is bound to one mission per game instance.

## Handler Recruitment (Alternative Method)

For smaller or more controlled games, the Handler may recruit agents individually:

1. The Handler recruits agents one at a time.
2. Each recruited player receives:
   - One mission
   - A rules summary
   - A warning that secrecy is essential
3. Agents may not openly discuss missions.
4. The Handler assigns a handle or allows the player to choose one.

## After Accepting a Mission

Regardless of intake method:

- Agents may not openly discuss their missions.
- Handles should be kept private until the game concludes.
- If a player loses their mission briefing, they must contact the Handler privately to recover it — not ask other agents.

---

# Winning the Game

Agents score points by completing missions while preserving cover.

## Standard Scoring

- Successful mission completion: +3 points
- Stylish or difficult completion bonus: +1 point
- Exposing another agent: +2 points
- Surviving the entire event without exposure: +2 points
- Having your cover blown: -3 points
- Revealing your mission intentionally: automatic mission failure

---

# Mission Design Rules

A mission should:

- Be performable in public
- Look plausibly normal to outsiders
- Encourage interaction or creativity
- Have objective proof requirements
- Avoid harassment, danger, or property damage

## Good Mission Examples

- Convince three strangers to salute you
- Get photographed next to five different people wearing red hats
- Take a group photo with over 20 different strangers
- Get another person to place rubber duck in the center of a fountain
- Obtain a business card from someone dressed as a wizard

---

# Exposure System

Agents may attempt to expose other agents.

An exposure attempt succeeds if:

- The exposing agent submits photo or video evidence of the target completing a mission-like action, OR
- The exposing agent correctly identifies both:
  1. That the target is playing Secret Agent
  2. The general nature of the mission

A failed exposure attempt penalizes the accuser with -1 point.

Once exposed, the target agent becomes **Burned**.

---

# Burned Status

A Burned agent:

- May continue playing
- Cannot earn stealth bonuses
- Becomes eligible for Counterintel missions
- May attempt revenge exposures

---

# Counterintel Variant

Optional advanced mode.

Some agents secretly receive counterintelligence missions:

- Identify active agents
- Feed misinformation
- Trick agents into exposing themselves
- Intercept dead drops

---

# Evidence Rules

Evidence must clearly demonstrate mission success.

## Acceptable Evidence

- Photographs
- Short video clips
- Physical objects

Evidence cannot:

- Endanger participants
- Violate venue rules
- Include harassment or non-consensual humiliation

---

# Borrowed Design DNA (Without Copying Existing Games)

The game intentionally draws inspiration from:

- Assassin-style elimination games
- Live-action roleplay secrecy systems
- Social deduction mechanics
- Scavenger hunt structures
- Spy fiction tradecraft

However, Secret Agent differs because:

- Participation itself is concealed
- Exposure is a scoring mechanic
- Missions are socially camouflaged
- The game rewards plausible deniability over elimination

# Quick Rules Summary

1. Scan the QR code, choose a handle, accept a mission.
2. Keep the game secret.
3. Complete missions without exposing yourself.
4. Gather proof.
5. Expose rival agents when possible.
6. Stay believable.
7. Respect safety rules.
8. The Handler's ruling is final.
